# Cybersecurity Researcher Roadmap

A Cybersecurity Researcher investigates the unknown. Whether working in an academic lab, a government agency, or a private "Think Tank," researchers focus on uncovering zero-day vulnerabilities, analyzing APT (Advanced Persistent Threat) behaviors, and developing future-proof security architectures.

## üü¢ Level 1: Foundational Literacy & Curiosity
Before you can research, you must master the fundamental building blocks of technology.
* **Computer Science Core:** Data structures, algorithms, and discrete mathematics.
* **OS Internals:** Deep understanding of how kernels, memory management, and file systems work.
* **Networking Protocols:** Moving beyond the basics to understand protocol design and packet manipulation.
* **Academic Writing:** Learning how to read and write technical whitepapers and peer-reviewed articles.

---

## üü° Level 2: Research Methodology
Research is not just "hacking"; it is a systematic process of discovery.
* **Literature Review:** Learning to navigate databases like Google Scholar, IEEE Xplore, and ACM Digital Library.
* **Experimental Design:** Setting up controlled experiments, using honeypots for data collection, and ensuring reproducibility.
* **Quantitative & Qualitative Analysis:** Using statistics to validate your security findings.
* **Responsible Disclosure:** Understanding the ethical and legal frameworks for reporting vulnerabilities (CVE process).



---

## üü† Level 3: Specialized Research Domains
Researchers often specialize in one "niche" area to become world-leading experts.
* **Vulnerability Research:** Finding zero-days in software, browsers, or kernel drivers.
* **Cryptography Research:** Analyzing encryption algorithms and searching for mathematical weaknesses.
* **Hardware Security:** Researching side-channel attacks, Rowhammer, or hardware Trojans.
* **Protocol Research:** Identifying flaws in the design of protocols like BGP, 5G, or TLS 1.3.

---

## üî¥ Level 4: The Intersection of AI and Security
The modern frontier of research involves automation and intelligent systems.
* **AI-Driven Threat Analysis:** Developing models that can predict attacks before they happen.
* **Securing the AI Supply Chain:** Researching the security of model weights and training datasets.
* **Automated Exploit Generation:** Using machine learning to find and patch bugs automatically.
